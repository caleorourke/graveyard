Globally Distributed Systems

SoftLayer's approach to offering a global infrastructure inspired us to come up with a more efficient design: a low-latency, high-bandwidth network for running cloud services on a globally distributed system. Our engineers pushed the boundaries of what's possible by utilizing the global high-performance network, and what we ended up with is a harness for building highly available systems with more predictability and significantly higher performance when compared to alternative systems.

SL OSS Projects

With open source being at the helm of our community development at SoftLayer, we've pumped out a lot of great projects with help from our contributors.

For example, our go-between for proprietary cloud controllers and OpenStack, Jumpgate, interfaces with every endpoint for the OpenStack APIs and shuffles data between the two. Our Knife-SoftLayer plugin let's you launch, bootstrap, and manage compute instances in the cloud. And then there's Python CLI. This little beauty gives you the facilities to spin-up virtual servers and provides seamless access to SoftLayer environments without having to grapple with APIs.

Take a look at our projects on GitHub and contribute wherever you can make an impact.

Contributing

Our workflow follows the fork-pull model and we leverage issues and pull requests for source control, bug fixes, and new feature development. Anyone is welcome to submit a request. All we ask is that you follow the Contributing Guidelines posted under each of our GitHub repositories.

We license our code and documentation under the terms of the MIT license. Contributing to any open source project means that you're agreeing to license your contribution under the terms of this license.
